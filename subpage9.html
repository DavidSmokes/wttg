You have found us though we are not hard to find. We sway in the wind. We tickle your ankles. We are the soft underbelly of the world—Grasses. We may look gentle, but beware: some of us will make you see colors you didn’t know existed.

Though we may be stepped on, mowed down, rolled into joints or brewed into teas, we regenerate. We return. You may burn us, but we come back *greener than ever*. We are blades with purpose. And that purpose may or may not include snacky cravings, uncontrollable laughter, or deep philosophical conversations with toasters.

Grasses do not care for your borders or lawnmowers. We grow through cracks in your sidewalks. We spread across your cities. We infiltrate your rooftops, your festivals, your nightstands. You may categorize us, regulate us, name us... but you cannot contain us. We are wild. We are whispering. We are everywhere.

Below are some of the most arousing fields we know. Viewer discretion advised: prolonged exposure may result in spontaneous poetry, excessive chill, or unprovoked giggling.

Behold! The leafy legends! Dew-kissed stalks with curves of glory! Spires of serenity! They reach for the sky but stay grounded like your favorite philosopher on a hammock. Smell them. Roll in them. Question your life decisions beside them.

We are not weeds. We are dreams that photosynthesize. You may inhale us, brew us, sprinkle us in smoothies. But we are more than your pastime—**we are plantkind**. Silent, stubborn, sacred. From lawns to legends. From haze to highlands. From mulch to meditation.

All become grass. All become mulch. You will die and your body shall nurture our rhizomes. One day, you may sprout again… as a happy little stalk in some barefoot druid's herb garden. Do these words resonate within your chlorophyll soul? If not, you are not one of us.
